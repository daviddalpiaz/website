Home | David Dalpiaz

Hello and welcome to my webzone! I’m currently an Associated Assistant Professor and Data Analytics Program Specialist for the Department of Statistics at The Ohio State University.

After a BS in Mathematics from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, I did a PhD in Statistics at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign with Ping Ma. I then spent four years as a Visiting Assistant Professor, then Lecturer and MS Academic Advisor, at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. I am now an Adjunct Teaching Assistant Professor for the Department of Statistics at the University of Illinois where my course Statistical Modeling in R is part of the online Master of Computer Science in Data Science. Yes, I spent a lot of time at UIUC. I’m still mad they don’t use UIUC anymore. Oh well. Go Illini!

Writing

  • Applied Statistics with R
    • Still a work in progress, but the content coverage is more or less solidified. Provides an introduction to both R and regression modeling for students with undergraduate experience in probability and statistics. Currently in use for STAT 420 both in-person and online at UIUC for the Departments of Statistics and Computer Science.
  • R for Statistical Learning
    • Currently undergoing considerable changes and developments. Originally intended to be supplemental notes to ISL in order to provide additional R code and rmarkdown examples, the text is starting to become a complete reference text for STAT 432 - Basics of Statistical Learning at UIUC for the Departments of Statistics. Target audience is advanced undergraduate students in statistics with previous experience with R and regression.
